About This Issue

Tourism brings money, jobs, and cultural exchange. But in some of the world's most visited cities, local residents are beginning to ask whether success has gone too far.

This issue helps learners explore both sides of the debate: the benefits of travel and the growing pressure on housing, public spaces, and local communities.

From the Article

In recent years, Barcelona has seen something strange. Some local residents have walked through crowded tourist districts carrying water pistols, spraying visitors as a form of protest. It was not meant as a joke. For many people who live there, tourism has become part of a bigger question: who really owns a city?
